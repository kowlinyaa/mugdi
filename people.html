MUGDI

Mugdi has a population of over 2,000, although the 2011 census reported only 819 residents, which is far from the current reality. The population has increased significantly in recent years, mainly due to migration, and some new areas have recently been populated. The 2011 census report will be provided below, along with an estimated population report based on more recent observations. According to ground reports, almost all families in the village now have their own houses.
Agriculture plays a major role in the village’s livelihood, with nearly 70% of the population depending on farming, either directly or indirectly. Some residents work on their own fields, while others work on neighboring farms in exchange for wages or a portion of the harvest. The village is also home to many educational institutions, and the literacy rate is steadily increasing. In terms of healthcare, Mugdi is equipped with good facilities to support the well-being of its residents.
The village is rich in natural resources as well. Many families own private ponds, and the total number of ponds is estimated to be over 100. Additionally, almost 90% of households rear animals such as cows, ducks, or hens, contributing to their livelihoods and food security. These features highlight the self-sustaining and community-oriented nature of Mugdi, making it a thriving rural settlement.

Census 2011

Here's a detailed summary of the Basic Population Details of Mugdi village:

~The total population of Mugdi village is around 819, including approximately 396 males and 423 females, with a sex ratio of 1068 females per 1,000 males.
~There are about 96 children aged 0–6 years in Mugdi village, reflecting the young population in the village.
~Mugdi village has 11 people belonging to the Scheduled Castes (SC) and 111 residents from the Scheduled Tribes (ST).
~The literacy rate of Mugdi village is about 68.01%, with male literacy at 73.23% and female literacy at 63.12%.
~There are around 157 households in Mugdi village.
